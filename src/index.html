What is Aerospace Jam?

A hands-on competition designed to inspire the next generation of engineers, programmers, and innovators.

Aerospace Jam challenges high school teams to design, build, and program a robot to solve a complex problem inspired by current aerospace missions. This year's theme is "Martian Soil Collection".

Teams will compete in a dynamic arena, simulating the harsh and unpredictable environment of the Red Planet. It's not just about winning; it's about problem-solving, collaboration, and pushing the boundaries of what's possible with technology.

Why Participate?

Gain invaluable skills, meet industry mentors, and have an unforgettable experience.

Real-World Skills

Develop your coding, engineering, and design skills by working on a project with real-world applications.

Teamwork & Collaboration

Learn to work effectively in a team, manage a project, and communicate complex ideas with your peers.

Industry Connections

Network with professional engineers and recruiters from leading aerospace and tech companies.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the maximum team size? +

Teams can have a minimum of 3 and a maximum of 5 student members, plus one adult mentor (teacher or parent).

Is there a specific robotics kit we need to use? +

No! Aerospace Jam is platform-agnostic. You can use any kit (VEX, REV, LEGO, etc.) or build from scratch, as long as your robot complies with the size, weight, and material constraints outlined in the official game manual.
